AI Agent: Practical Guide for Everyday Users

Get more from your data with AI-powered insights

As someone who regularly works with data, you already know how valuable your information is. An AI Agent helps you unlock deeper insights from that data through simple, conversation-based interactions. This guide will show you how to get the most helpful information from your AI assistant.

How Your AI Agent Works With Existing Data

Your AI Agent connects to all the data you're already collecting. This typically includes:

Event & Activity Data

Dates, times, schedules, and associated details

Form Responses & Recaps

All your form fields and responses from completed activities

Team Information

Assignments, hours, roles, and performance data

Locations & Geography

Venue types, locations, and regional organization

Programs & Categories

All the categorization you use for grouping and classification

Qualitative Feedback

Comments, notes, and text-based responses

Everyday Tips for Better Results

These simple strategies will help you get the most useful information:

  • Be specific about timeframes: "Show me data from January to March" or "last 30 days" helps focus your results
  • Name your programs and categories: "Show me data for our Sampling Program" works better than "Show me data by program"
  • Specify locations or regions whenever possible â€” this dramatically improves relevance
  • Ask follow-up questions to drill down into interesting points ("Can you break that down by venue type?")
  • Request specific formats when needed ("Show this as a chart" or "Create a comparison table")
  • Provide feedback if the Agent misunderstands ("That's not what I meant" or "Please summarize this")
Pro Tip: Combining program AND location specifications in your queries will give you the most focused, relevant results. For example: "Show me our Summer Program performance in the Northeast Region for Q2" is much better than "Show me our program performance."

The Conversational Approach

Think of your AI Agent as a conversation with a helpful colleague who knows your data really well. You can:

  • Start specific: Begin with a focused question about a specific program and market
  • Ask follow-up questions: "That's interesting. Can you tell me more about why that market performed better?"
  • Request changes: "Can you show that same data broken down by month instead of by region?"
  • Get explanations: "Why did we see such a significant drop in performance in February?"

This conversational approach often leads to insights you might not have thought to look for initially.

You

That's interesting about Chicago. What factors contributed to their success?

Would you like a detailed analysis of any of these factors?
You
Yes, analyze the impact of weekend vs. weekday events across all Midwest markets.
AI Agent
Weekend events consistently outperform weekday events with 37% higher attendance and 22% better conversion rates across all Midwest markets. Chicago had the highest percentage of weekend events, which appears to be a significant factor in their success. Would you like this as a chart for your reporting?
